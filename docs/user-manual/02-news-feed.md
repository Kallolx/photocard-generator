# Today's News Feed

## Your Personal News Hub

The **Today's News** feature brings the latest headlines from popular news sources directly into SocialCard. Instead of visiting dozens of websites, you can browse everything in one place — and instantly turn any headline into a professional social media card.

> 🔒 **Plan Requirement**: The News Feed is available for **Premium** users only. Free users will see an upgrade prompt.

---

## How It Works

1. **Open Today's News** from the sidebar or Dashboard
2. SocialCard automatically fetches the latest news from dozens of sources
3. Browse headlines, read snippets, and see article images
4. Click any article to create a card from it

---

## What You See on the News Page

### News Stats Bar
At the top, you'll see three key numbers:
- **Articles Today** — Total number of news articles available
- **Active Sources** — How many news outlets are included
- **Last Updated** — When the feed was last refreshed (with a refresh button)

### Filter Controls
Below the stats, you have powerful filters:

| Filter | What It Does |
|---|---|
| **Source Filter** | Show news from a specific outlet (e.g., Prothom Alo, BBC, etc.) |
| **Category Filter** | Filter by topic (Politics, Sports, Technology, etc.) |
| **Region Filter** | Choose by geography — Bangladesh, Global, Asia, Europe, USA |

### Search
Use the search bar in the header to quickly find articles by keyword.

---

## Browsing News Articles

Each news article appears as a card with:
- **Thumbnail Image** — The article's main photo
- **Source Badge** — The news outlet's name with its favicon
- **Time Stamp** — How long ago the article was published (e.g., "2 hours ago")
- **Headline** — The article title
- **Description** — A short preview of the article content
- **Original Link** — Hover over the image to see a link icon; click it to read the full article on the original website

### Pagination
Articles are shown 24 per page. Use the page controls at the bottom to navigate between pages.

---

## Reading Article Details

Click the **"Details"** button on any article card to see:
- The full article content (when available)
- A larger image preview
- Quick action buttons to create a card

---

## Creating a Card from a News Article

This is where the magic happens! From any news article, you can:

### Quick Create
1. Click the **"Create Card"** button on any article
2. A theme menu appears — choose your preferred theme:
   - **Classic** — Traditional, structured layout
   - **Overlay** — Text overlaid on the image
   - **Source** — Source-focused design
   - **Banner** — Wide banner layout
   - **Minimal** — Clean, minimal design
   - **Blend** — Modern gradient design
   - **Duo** — Dual-tone design
3. Click your chosen theme
4. You'll be taken directly to the **URL Newscard editor** with the article already loaded and the theme applied!

### What Gets Transferred
When you create a card from the news feed, SocialCard automatically sends:
- ✅ The article URL
- ✅ Your chosen theme
- ✅ The headline text
- ✅ The article content (for AI features)
- ✅ The article image

You won't need to copy-paste anything!

---

## Refreshing the Feed

The news feed caches articles for 5 minutes to keep things fast. To get the absolute latest headlines:

1. Find the **Last Updated** timestamp in the stats bar
2. Click the **Refresh** button (the circular arrow icon)
3. Wait a moment while fresh headlines are loaded

---

## Language Support

The news feed fully supports both **English** and **Bengali (Bangla)** content:
- Bengali headlines display in the proper **Noto Serif Bengali** font
- Bengali source names show correctly
- The system automatically detects the language and adjusts the font

---

## Tips for Power Users

1. **Use region filters to find diverse content** — Switch between Bangladesh, Global, and Asia to find stories from different perspectives

2. **Filter by source first** — If you always create cards from a specific news outlet, use the source filter to quickly find their articles

3. **Create multiple cards at once** — Open several articles in different themes to quickly build a day's worth of social media content

4. **Check the time stamps** — More recent articles tend to perform better on social media

5. **Use the search** — Looking for a specific topic? Search by keyword to find relevant articles instantly
